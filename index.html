About

Hi, I'm Danson — the result of mixing tech, capital, and a slightly irrational fear of irrelevance.

On paper, that's a background across computer science, finance, and AI. None of it deep enough to call myself a specialist — I know where that gap is. What I do have is the habit of taking something complicated, looking for the system underneath it, and asking where the value actually sits.

I lean on evidence over instinct, though I've learned my instincts tend to run ahead of my reasoning — I'll see where something should go long before I can show my work, which is useful and dangerous in roughly equal measure. I like being argued with. I've always thought numbers tell one story and people tell another, and the interesting part is usually where the two disagree.

What I'm after is range. Markets, product design, systems, the odd science of getting a human body to work better — I'd rather understand ten things well enough to connect them than one thing so well I can't talk about anything else. My world is deliberately international; most of the best ideas I've run into arrived through people, over a table, rarely on schedule.

I'm always up for good ideas, good people, or questionable plans that just might work. If you're carrying any of the three, this page ends with my email.

What I work on

Less a job description than the handful of things I actually spend time on — and, in a couple of cases, am still trying to get good at.

01Investing & capital allocation

Running a small portfolio with more process than its size strictly warrants — written theses, deliberate sizing, honest post-mortems. Mostly long-term and institutional in approach, with smaller sleeves I treat frankly as tuition.

02Financial analysis & tooling

Building tools that put AI to work on real financial analysis — turning filings, reports and messy data into something a decision-maker can actually use. It's where the finance and the technical side finally do something together.

03Systems & research

The connective work: digging into a problem until the picture holds up, finding the one variable quietly driving it, then making the case plainly enough to act on.

04Building & ownership

Slowly working toward owning things I understand rather than just renting my time to them. Early, imperfect, and the part I'm most deliberately bad-then-better at.
